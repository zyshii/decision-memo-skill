# Interview Question Bank

Use these questions during Step 3 of the workflow. Ask only what's missing — not the whole list. Cap at 3 questions per turn.

## Meta Questions (ask once at start)

- "What project is this from?" — determines Notion location and ID prefix
- "How important was this decision?" — calibrates the retrospective depth (High/Medium/Low)
- "What was your role?" — decision-maker, proposer, executor, observer (this shapes Part B framing)

---

## Section 1 — Context

**Always ask:**
- "What triggered this — was there a specific event, or a pattern that accumulated?"
- "What constraints were you working inside? Time, resources, compliance, politics?"

**Ask if decision sits in a larger organizational dynamic:**
- "Are there structural factors here — team composition, reporting lines, tenure — that shaped the decision space?"
- "Would someone in a different role or tenure have faced the same constraints?"

**Ask if information was incomplete:**
- "What did you wish you'd known but didn't? How did you decide anyway?"

---

## Section 2 — Options Evaluated (critical gate)

**Always ask (do not skip):**
- "What alternatives did you consider before landing on this?"
- "What was the second-best option, and why didn't you pick it?"

**If user only described one option:**
- "Was there really only one path forward? Or were there alternatives you ruled out so fast they didn't feel like options?"
- "If nothing was ruled out, this isn't really a decision memo — it's an execution memo. Do we want to capture it differently, or dig for the choice point?"

**Probing the tradeoff:**
- "Every decision sits between two values you had to weigh. What were yours here?"
- "What would you have had to give up if you'd chosen differently?"

---

## Section 3 — Stakeholder Dynamics

**Who was involved:**
- "Who supported this? What was their motivation — aligned with users, aligned with business, or something else?"
- "Who pushed back? What was their reasoning, and how did you engage with it?"
- "Anyone neutral or watching from the sidelines who mattered?"

**How the user navigated:**
- "What did you do to move the people who weren't with you?"
- "Did you package the decision differently for different audiences? Why?"
- "Were there conversations where you picked your words carefully? What was the political read there?"

**Unresolved tension (easy to skip, high-signal):**
- "Is there anyone who still doesn't agree with how this went? What did you do with that?"
- "Did any disagreement just get shelved rather than resolved?"

**Political judgment (Part A only, never Part B):**
- "Did you make any calls that weren't about the design/merits — preserving relationships, picking battles, conserving capital?"
- "Looking back, was there a moment you could have pushed harder but chose not to? What was the calculation?"

---

## Section 4 — Decision Process

**Method:**
- "How did you actually decide — data, research, gut, pattern recognition, consensus?"
- "How long did it take from recognizing the problem to landing on an answer?"

**Authority:**
- "Who had final say? You, a PM, leadership, the team?"
- "Where was your actual influence — were you deciding, proposing, or executing?"

**Meta-reflection:**
- "If you could redo the *process* (not the decision itself), what would you change?"
- "Did you have the right method for the stakes?"

---

## Section 5 — Outcome (lightweight)

**After decision has landed:**
- "What's happened since? Any signal on whether it worked?"
- "Quantitative signals if any — metrics, usage, feedback volume?"
- "Qualitative — how do stakeholders talk about it now?"
- "Did it land the way you expected? Where did reality diverge from prediction?"

**If outcome is still forming:**
- "What's the earliest signal you're watching for? How will you know it's working or not?"

---

## Section 6 — Surprises & Lessons (the senior-level differentiator)

**What the user got wrong:**
- "What did you predict that turned out to be wrong?"
- "What assumption going in was off?"

**Second-order effects:**
- "What did this solve that also created something new?"
- "Did any relationships or dynamics shift because of how this played out?"

**Transferable heuristics:**
- "If you faced a structurally similar situation tomorrow, what would you carry forward?"
- "What's the one-sentence lesson a younger you would have benefited from hearing?"
- "What's a heuristic from this that would apply even in a totally different org or project?"

---

## Part B Extraction Questions

After Part A is drafted, before writing Part B:

- "What kinds of BQ questions would this story best answer?" — helps position the STAR framing
- "What's the hardest follow-up a tough interviewer could ask? Let's prepare for it."
- "Is there anything in Part A that absolutely cannot make it to Part B? Names, company specifics, things you'd regret saying externally?"

---

## Quick Capture Mode — Minimum Viable Interview

When user picks Quick mode, ask only:

1. "What's the project and the decision in one sentence?"
2. "What were the 2-3 options, and which did you pick?"
3. "What was the key tradeoff?"
4. "How did it turn out?"
5. "One-sentence lesson?"
6. "What BQ question does this best answer?"

Skip the reframing check in Quick mode — assume the user has already internally framed it.
